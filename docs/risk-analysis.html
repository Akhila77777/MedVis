Risk Analysis — study version

An ISO 14971-style hazard analysis of the NeedlePathPlanner needle-planning workflow. Companion to the canonical hazard-analysis.md and Module 6 of the learning guide.

Honesty note. This is a learning-scale hazard analysis, not a regulatory risk management file — no risk policy, no clinical data, no formal review. It demonstrates ISO 14971 thinking (the hazard chain, risk controls, residual risk, traceability), and it covers software-related hazards only.

1. The risk chain (the core mental model)

ISO 14971 keeps three words strictly separate — risk controls attack different links:

Hazard  →  (sequence of events)  →  Hazardous situation  →  Harm
TermMeaningExample (this tool)
HazardPotential source of harm (a condition)The planned path passes through a vessel
Hazardous situationA person is exposed to the hazardClinician advances the needle believing it clear
HarmThe actual injuryVessel laceration, internal haemorrhage

2. Risk estimation scales

Severity (S): 1 Negligible · 2 Minor · 3 Serious · 4 Critical · 5 Catastrophic.
Probability (P): 1 Improbable · 2 Remote · 3 Occasional · 4 Probable · 5 Frequent.
Risk = S × P: 1–4 Low · 5–9 Medium · 10–25 High.

Software probability caveat. A software fault is systematic, not random — you can't "probability-rate a bug" the way you rate a resistor's failure. So P here is the worst-case likelihood that the hazardous situation reaches harm given the controls (including the human-in-the-loop). This is exactly why IEC 62304's software safety class is driven by severity, not a failure rate.

3. Risk-control hierarchy (ISO 14971 §7 — priority order)

  1. Inherently safe design — design the hazard out. (REQ-006 computes the intersection automatically.)
  2. Protective measures — guards/alarms. (REQ-009 never shows a false "Clear"; REQ-018 errors instead of failing silently.)
  3. Information for safety — labels, warnings, training. (REQ-008 colour-coding, REQ-010 naming the segment.) Weakest tier — a warning can be ignored.

Control hierarchy: ① inherently safe design · ② protective measure · ③ information for safety · ext external/process.

Interview angle — why H-04 stays Medium. Its controls are almost all information for safety (the weakest tier). You cannot mitigate a use-error away with another label — it needs a formal IEC 62366-1 usability study and a redundant indicator (text + icon + colour). Saying that honestly shows you understand the control hierarchy.

5. Residual risk summary

After controls, H-01/H-02/H-03 reduce to Low–Medium and H-04 remains Medium. Every residual risk depends partly on the mandatory human-in-the-loop — the device is a planning aid; the radiologist reviews the plan and uses independent intra-procedure imaging. On that basis the residual risks are judged acceptable for this learning-scale tool, given the benefit of catching vessel-crossing trajectories before insertion. The H-02 (re-plan recompute) and H-03 (missing-point and empty-segmentation errors) controls are now covered by automated regression tests in NeedlePathPlannerTest, so they stay verified as the code evolves.

6. Closing the loop (the payoff)

The whole project becomes one connected argument:

ISO 14971 hazard (vessel puncture) → risk control → REQ-006 "determine intersection" (Module 5) → "Verify clearance" function (Module 4) → checkClearance() + its unit tests (Module 3).

Hazard to line of code, and back — traceable in both directions (IEC 62304).

7. Standards reference (for study)

ISO 14971 — risk management

The standard behind this whole page. Identify hazards → estimate & evaluate risk → implement risk controls (in the §7 hierarchy) → evaluate residual risk → weigh against benefit. The output is the risk management file this table emulates. ISO/TR 24971 is the official guidance with worked examples.

IEC 62304 — medical device software lifecycle

Software items that can contribute to a hazardous situation get a safety class (A/B/C) driven by severity; software risk control measures must be specified and verified (§7) and traced. Our Class B decision comes from this analysis.

IEC 62366-1 — usability engineering

Owns H-04: harm caused by use error, not a code fault. A real device file would run formative/summative usability evaluations to justify the residual risk of a misread result.

EU MDR 2017/745

Requires a documented risk management system across the lifecycle (Annex I GSPR §3) and that risks be reduced as far as possible. ISO 14971 is the harmonised standard used to demonstrate this.
